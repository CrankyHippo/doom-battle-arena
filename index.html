Battle Arena for Final Doom

Battle Arena is a single-level modification for Final Doom: TNT – Evilution, built as a long-form co-op playground. Defend a UAC research facility, hold the bunkers, unleash demons from the BRIG, and role-play Marines, workers, or SWAT during outbreak scenarios.

Story & Origins

Battle Arena exists both as an in-universe UAC black-ops facility and as a passion project built over years of late-night Doom co-op. This section covers both sides of that story.

In-Universe: UAC Battle Research Center

The Battle Arena is a secret Union Aerospace Corporation complex on Earth. Its purpose: capture and study creatures discovered via ancient teleporters on Mars’ moons – then throw elite Marines at them until someone learns something or dies trying.

The facility is split between a biological research wing – packed with caged monsters – and the Arena itself, where live-fire tests pit Marines against demonic hordes. Safety is “a top priority”, at least according to the welcome briefing you get before stepping into a building full of teleporters, experimental power systems, and a room literally called the BRIG.

Real-World: Two High-School Doom Nerds

Battle Arena started life around 2002–2004 when Alex “Anelf3” Gumpel and Bryan “CrankyHippo” Burnett tried to recreate their favorite Doom II memory: sprinting past a Cyberdemon that slowly crept closer to the spawn every time they died.

The original prototype was just a giant pit full of Cyberdemons and a central bunker. From there the level grew organically: control room, exterior courtyard, BRIG, armory, med bay, maintenance shafts, and a spawn zone where players choose how to enter the map.

The user’s guide was written so people wouldn’t just load BA as a normal map, beat it, and move on. It’s meant to be a sandbox you live in for a while.

How to Play & Roles

Battle Arena shines when players adopt loose roles and lean into the scenarios: a normal day at work, sudden containment failures, and full-scale demon outbreaks.

Requirements & Setup

  • Base game: Final Doom – TNT: Evilution (TNT.WAD)
  • PWAD: BA.WAD (this map)
  • Recommended port: Doom Legacy (Software renderer, MIDI disabled)
  • Recommended lobby: Doom Connector for easy multiplayer rooms

Typical multiplayer setup (Doom Legacy/Doom Connector):

  • Game mode: Cooperative
  • Skill: Nightmare (for the intended chaos)
  • Team damage: On (friendly fire is part of the fun)
  • Additional file: BA.WAD

Roles

Marine (Gray) Worker / Scientist SWAT (Red)
  • Marines spawn at the front entrance or directly in the Arena, gear up in the Armory, and fight demons until they’re out of blood or ammo. They’re the stars of the show in the Arena.
  • Workers / Scientists keep the place running: starting at the entrance or Control Room, watching Marines from behind glass, running to Engineering or Medical, or occasionally picking up a gun when things go bad.
  • SWAT is only called when something has gone very wrong: monsters loose in the corridors, a Marine snapping into “space madness”, or a BRIG malfunction. They use a red keycard to access a private SWAT entrance and high-speed elevator into the maintenance shafts.

House rule: to “use the map” or check cameras, you’re supposed to be standing in front of a computer screen in-game. It makes getting lost in the maintenance shafts a lot more tense.

Scenarios

  • Normal Day at Work – Everyone spawns via the front entrance, checks in at Security, hits the Armory, and then activates the Arena from the main bunker. Retreat to Medical and restock when needed.
  • Containment Failure – The BRIG cells open and demons spill into the main corridor. Workers dive into maintenance shafts; Marines & SWAT scramble to seal the area and clear the base.
  • Space Madness – One Marine “snaps” and becomes a hostile player. Workers hide and track them on cameras while SWAT hunts them through the shafts.
  • Cyberdemon Loose in the Base – Boss-tier emergency. Bunkers, teleports, and SWAT entrance all come into play while everyone tries not to get vaporized.

Base & Infrastructure

  • Spawn Area – Purgatory room with teleporters: front entrance, Control Room, or straight into the Arena.
  • Courtyard & Main Entrance Gate – Exterior area with a fountain and benches; the “normal” way into work at BA.
  • Security Checkpoint & Elevator – Main vertical access; baggage scans, sign-in, and weapon checks.
  • Main Corridor – Central hallway linking all major rooms. Each door has its own symbol for quick identification.
  • Control Room – Overlooks the Arena; monitors Marine vitals, power, and security. Contains BRIG release switches and an emergency drop into the Arena.
  • Engineering – Power systems and control panels that keep the facility and Arena running.
  • Maintenance Shafts – Narrow back-corridors behind every room. Monsters can’t fit; humans can. Perfect for shortcuts and escape routes.
  • Emergency Exit – Theoretical route out of the level, optionally disabled in multiplayer settings.

Combat Zones & Bunkers

  • BRIG – Holding pens for “pig” demons used for research and as a source of monsters during outbreaks.
  • Armory – Wall-to-wall guns, ammo, armor and maps. Standard stop before entering the Arena.
  • Medical Bay – Where badly wounded Marines get patched up between rounds.
  • Teleporter Room – Teleport pads to key areas and corner bunkers. Great for fast insertions; risky if someone is standing on the destination pad.
  • Spare Room – Unfinished expansion space, rumored to be a future mess hall.
  • The Arena & Elevators – Main kill zone where demons are released and Marines fight for as long as they can.
  • Main Bunker – Central fortified structure inside the Arena. Has shutters, shield controls, a medkit and a teleporter back to base.
  • Corner Bunkers – Four smaller bunkers scattered around the Arena. Good for catching your breath, not great for long sieges.
  • SWAT Emergency Entrance & Elevator – Red-key-only access point with a high-speed lift straight into the maintenance shafts for SWAT teams.
